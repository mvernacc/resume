CFS's existential bet is building a tokamak (fusion device) with a better superconductor (HTS) in its superconducting electromagnets to produce stronger magnetic fields and more fusion power (power ~ field4). When we started, the world's biggest HTS magnet fit on a dinner plate; now we build multiple truck-sized magnets/month. Magnets must operate with tight margin in difficult-to-test conditions (20 kelvin, 20 tesla). My team makes software to design and build those magnets.

Manufacturing optimization software enables CFS to utilize a large fraction of the world's HTS production, ~$100M/year, by yielding consistent magnet performance while accepting a wide range of superconductor quality. Helped increase magnet production rate >10x. Optimizer allocates superconductor to magnets (Python); web app automates & stores superconductor acceptance tests (TypeScript, Rust). Magnet production would halt without this software.
